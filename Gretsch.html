About Gretsch

Gretsch Guitar Company is a historic and highly respected manufacturer of guitars, drums, and other musical instruments, known for its innovative designs and unique sound. Founded in 1883 by Friedrich Gretsch in Brooklyn, New York, the company initially produced banjos, tambourines, and drums. After Friedrich's passing, his son Fred Gretsch Sr. took over the business, expanding its offerings and transforming Gretsch into one of the most prominent names in the musical instrument industry during the early 20th century.

In the 1930s and 1940s, Gretsch began focusing on guitars, introducing high-quality archtop models that gained popularity among jazz musicians. By the 1950s, the company became synonymous with rockabilly and early rock 'n' roll thanks to its collaboration with artists like Chet Atkins, who helped design signature models such as the Gretsch 6120 and Country Gentleman. These guitars featured stylish aesthetics, innovative pickups, and a rich, twangy tone that became integral to the sound of the era. Gretsch’s “Filter’Tron” pickups, introduced in the mid-1950s, set a new standard for clarity and output.

During the 1960s, Gretsch guitars were prominently used by influential musicians such as George Harrison of The Beatles, who played models like the Gretsch Duo Jet and Country Gentleman. The brand’s iconic designs, including hollow-body and semi-hollow guitars with distinctive finishes and hardware, cemented its reputation as a leader in quality and style. Gretsch also gained recognition for its drum kits, which were used by many prominent drummers during the same period.

However, the company faced challenges in the late 1960s and 1970s after being sold to Baldwin Piano Company, which led to a decline in quality and popularity. Gretsch struggled to compete in the changing music market, and its guitars became less prominent. In 1985, Fred Gretsch III, a descendant of the company’s founders, regained control of the brand and began restoring its reputation by reviving classic designs and improving production standards.

In 2002, Gretsch entered into a strategic partnership with Fender Musical Instruments Corporation, allowing Fender to handle manufacturing and distribution while Fred Gretsch III maintained ownership. This collaboration helped reintroduce Gretsch guitars to a new generation of players, combining modern innovations with the brand’s vintage charm. Gretsch reissued many of its classic models, updated with improved electronics and build quality while preserving their retro aesthetic and iconic tone.

Today, Gretsch is celebrated for its rich legacy and continues to produce a wide range of guitars, from affordable entry-level models to high-end professional instruments. Its hollow-body and semi-hollow designs remain especially popular in genres like rockabilly, jazz, country, and indie rock. Gretsch's commitment to craftsmanship and innovation has ensured its place as a cornerstone of the guitar industry for over a century.

Noteable Gretsch Products
  • Falcon

    The Gretsch Falcon is one of the most iconic and luxurious electric guitars in the music world, celebrated for its striking appearance and premium craftsmanship. Introduced in 1955, the Falcon was designed as a high-end showcase of Gretsch’s artistry, blending innovative features with opulent aesthetics. Its large hollow body, gold-plated hardware, and distinctive "winged" headstock logo set it apart as a symbol of sophistication and elegance. The guitar’s eye-catching design, including glittering inlays and unique finishes, has earned it a reputation as a "Cadillac of guitars."

    Beyond its visual appeal, the Gretsch Falcon is renowned for its warm, resonant tone and versatile sound. Its hollow-body construction, combined with Gretsch’s signature Filter’Tron or DynaSonic pickups (depending on the model), produces a bright, clear sound with excellent articulation, making it ideal for genres like rockabilly, jazz, country, and even modern rock. The Falcon’s tonal richness and sustain have made it a favorite among guitarists seeking both visual impact and exceptional performance.

    Over the decades, the Falcon has evolved through various iterations, including different body sizes, finishes, and electronic configurations, while maintaining its signature style. It has been played by renowned musicians such as Neil Young, Billy Duffy of The Cult, and Stephen Stills, solidifying its status as a classic. Today, the Gretsch Falcon remains a flagship model in the company’s lineup, available in vintage-inspired reissues and modern designs that continue to embody its legacy as one of the most prestigious guitars in the world.

  • Jet

    The Gretsch Jet is a renowned line of electric guitars known for its solid-body construction, classic tone, and stylish design. First introduced in the early 1950s, the Jet was Gretsch’s answer to the rising popularity of solid-body guitars, such as the Gibson Les Paul. Despite being marketed as a solid-body, the Gretsch Jet features a chambered body design, combining the sustain and focus of a solid guitar with the resonance and warmth of a hollow body. This unique construction has made the Jet a versatile instrument suitable for a wide range of musical genres.

    A key feature of the Gretsch Jet is its distinctive sound, achieved through Gretsch’s signature pickups, such as the DynaSonic single-coils or Filter’Tron humbuckers, depending on the model. These pickups produce a bright, clear tone with a twangy character that retains clarity even at high gain levels. Combined with the Jet’s lightweight body and comfortable neck profile, the guitar delivers a playing experience that appeals to both studio and live performers. Its elegant design, featuring vintage-style finishes and distinctive Gretsch aesthetics, further adds to its appeal.

    The Jet series has expanded over the decades to include a variety of models, including the Duo Jet, Silver Jet, and Jet Firebird, each catering to different stylistic preferences and tonal needs. Played by legendary artists such as George Harrison, Malcolm Young, and David Gilmour, the Gretsch Jet has solidified its place as an iconic guitar. Today, it remains a cornerstone of the Gretsch lineup, available in classic reissues as well as modern iterations, ensuring its legacy as a versatile and timeless instrument.

  • Penguin

    The Gretsch Penguin is one of the brand’s most distinctive and luxurious electric guitars, blending the opulence of the Gretsch Falcon with the compact, solid-body design of the Jet. Introduced in the 1950s as part of Gretsch’s high-end lineup, the Penguin is celebrated for its dazzling appearance and refined craftsmanship. Its unique design features gold-plated hardware, sparkling binding, and iconic hump-block inlays, all of which contribute to its reputation as one of the most elegant guitars in Gretsch's catalog. The Penguin’s sleek single-cutaway body and vintage-inspired finishes, such as White or Black with gold accents, make it a true statement piece.

    Sonically, the Penguin is as impressive as its looks, offering a versatile range of tones suitable for various musical genres. Equipped with Gretsch's signature Filter’Tron humbuckers, the Penguin produces a clear, articulate sound with a bright top end and balanced low frequencies. Its chambered mahogany body construction enhances resonance while maintaining the focused sustain of a solid-body guitar. This combination makes the Penguin ideal for players seeking a guitar that excels in everything from rockabilly and jazz to modern rock and pop.

    Over the years, the Gretsch Penguin has become a highly sought-after instrument for its rarity and status as a collector’s item. Its association with Gretsch’s heritage of quality and innovation has made it a favorite among discerning musicians and collectors alike. Today, the Penguin is available in both vintage-inspired reissues and contemporary variations, ensuring that its legacy as one of Gretsch’s most prestigious and striking guitars endures.

  • Broadkaster

    The Gretsch Broadkaster is a historic and highly regarded line of guitars, first introduced in the 1950s as part of the brand’s premium offering. The Broadkaster was designed as a high-end, versatile instrument that blended the tonal warmth of hollow-body construction with the clarity and sustain typically found in solid-body guitars. Known for its elegant design and powerful sound, the Broadkaster featured a large hollow body, distinctive F-holes, and high-quality materials, making it one of the most refined models in the Gretsch lineup during its era.

    Sonically, the Broadkaster is celebrated for its deep, resonant tones, thanks to its hollow-body construction, which allows for a rich, full-bodied sound. Equipped with Gretsch's famous Filter’Tron pickups, the Broadkaster delivers a unique blend of clarity and warmth, with a touch of twang that made it particularly popular with rockabilly, country, and jazz musicians. The guitar’s versatility is further enhanced by its smooth playability and wide tonal range, which allowed it to excel both in the studio and on stage. The Broadkaster’s combination of elegant craftsmanship and distinctive sound made it a favorite among professional musicians.

    While the original Gretsch Broadkaster was discontinued in the late 1960s, its legacy endures through reissues and the continued reverence for its design and tone. Today, the Broadkaster remains a sought-after instrument for collectors and players who appreciate its vintage charm and iconic sound. As part of Gretsch’s ongoing commitment to quality and innovation, the Broadkaster has become a symbol of the brand's legacy in producing guitars that blend style, craftsmanship, and exceptional performance.
